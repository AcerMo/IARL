Introduction

LIAL (LLM assisted Intention-Aligned Reinforcement Learning) is an innovative approach that combines Large Language Models (LLMs) with reinforcement learning to create agents that better align with human intentions. This project focuses on visualizing agent replays to better understand and analyze the behavior of these intention-aligned agents.

Our methodology integrates LLM-based intention modeling with traditional RL algorithms, allowing agents to not only maximize rewards but also align their behavior with specified intentions. This alignment is crucial for deploying AI systems in real-world scenarios where understanding and predictability are essential.

The visualization platform presented here allows researchers to examine agent behavior across various tasks and environments, providing insights into how intention alignment affects performance and decision-making processes.

Future Prospect

The LIAL framework opens up several promising directions for future research. Our immediate plans include extending the intention modeling capabilities to handle more complex and nuanced human instructions, potentially incorporating multimodal inputs beyond text.

We are also exploring ways to improve the sample efficiency of our approach, particularly in scenarios where interactions with the environment are costly or time-consuming. This includes investigating meta-learning techniques and few-shot adaptation strategies.

Long-term, we envision LIAL evolving into a comprehensive framework for developing AI systems that can reliably understand and execute complex human intentions across diverse domains, from robotics to decision support systems.

Another important direction is improving the interpretability of intention-aligned agents. We plan to develop visualization tools that not only show what the agent is doing but also why it's making specific decisions in relation to the given intentions.
